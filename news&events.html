Mistery Christmas Presents

Take part in the Mystery Christmas Presents and be the winner of great presents! Each person is allowed to pick one Christmas present from under the Christmas tree in the hallway. Discover many interesting surprises such as toys, art souvenirs and books. The event will take place between the 29th of November and 24th of December.

The entry to this event requires a Christmas Pack ticket which can be bought from the tickets kiosk at the entrance. The presents containing toys are marked with a green sticker. The money raised from our events are sent to a charity that helps children suffering from leukemia.

News Year fireworks

Witness the passing of 2021 by taking part in our New Year Fireworks event. A great display of light and colour awaits those who wish to spend this iconic night at the Stephenson's Historic House. The fireworks show is set to last at least 20 minutes and will be accompanied by music.

During the New Year's Eve only the garden and café are open until 4 AM. The money raised from our events are sent to a charity that helps children suffering from leukemia.

Rival railway museums in row over steam locomotive ownership

Britain led the world in the development of railways which acted as a stimulus for the Industrial Revolution by facilitating the transport of raw materials and manufactured goods. George Stephenson, with his work on the Stockton and Darlington Railway and the Liverpool and Manchester Railway, paved the way for the railway engineers who followed, such as his son Robert, his assistant Joseph Locke who carried out much work on his own account and Isambard Kingdom Brunel.
